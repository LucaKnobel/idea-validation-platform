# Documentation

Welcome to the product documentation.

The platform helps founders, startups, and innovation teams validate business ideas in a structured and repeatable way. Instead of investing significant time and budget in product development too early, critical assumptions can be tested first with real evidence.

## Quick Start

The typical validation process consists of six steps:

1. Create an idea
2. Complete the Business Model Canvas
3. Define hypotheses
4. Define metrics and experiments
5. Record results
6. Evaluate findings and improve the idea

The goal is not to build a product as quickly as possible. The goal is to learn as early as possible whether the core assumptions are valid.

## Core Concepts

### Idea

An idea is the starting point of the validation process.

It captures the problem, target context, and proposed business concept. Multiple versions can be created to document progress and directional changes over time.

### Business Model Canvas

The Business Model Canvas helps structure and document the most important aspects of a business idea.

The platform supports:

- Customer Segments
- Value Propositions
- Channels
- Customer Relationships
- Revenue Streams
- Key Resources
- Key Activities
- Key Partners
- Cost Structure

The canvas is the foundation for all validation activities.

### Hypotheses

Hypotheses are testable assumptions.

Example:

> Students are willing to pay CHF 5 per month for a digital study tool.

Good hypotheses are:

- specific
- testable
- measurable

The more critical an assumption is to the success of an idea, the more important it is to validate it.

### Metrics

Metrics define how success is measured.

Examples:

- Number of signups
- Conversion rate
- Interview acceptance rate
- Revenue
- Click-through rate

Every hypothesis should be linked to at least one measurable metric.

### Experiments

Experiments are used to test hypotheses.

Typical examples include:

- Customer interviews
- Surveys
- Landing pages
- Advertising campaigns
- Prototype tests

Experiments are planned and documented inside the platform but executed outside of it.

### Measurements

Measurements capture the outcomes of an experiment.

Examples:

- 25 signups
- 12 interview participants
- 9% conversion rate

The platform compares these outcomes against predefined success criteria.

### Validation Status

Each hypothesis has a validation status.

#### Not Tested

There is not enough evidence yet.

#### Validated

The predefined success criteria have been met.

#### Invalidated

The predefined success criteria have not been met.

The status gives teams a transparent view of the current confidence level.

### Versioning

The platform supports continuous idea development through versioning.

New versions make learning progress explicit and help teams separate different directions clearly.

#### Iteration

An iteration is used when the core idea remains promising.

Existing assumptions and learnings are carried forward. The goal is to improve the current direction.

#### Pivot

A pivot is used when key assumptions have been disproven and a new direction is required.

Validated and not-tested assumptions can be carried forward, while invalidated assumptions are intentionally excluded.

## Typical Workflow

```text
Create Idea
      ↓
Complete Canvas
      ↓
Define Hypotheses
      ↓
Define Metrics
      ↓
Plan Experiments
      ↓
Run Experiments
      ↓
Record Measurements
      ↓
Evaluate Hypotheses
      ↓
Iteration or Pivot
```

This cycle is repeated until there is enough evidence to support informed decisions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does the platform run experiments automatically?

No. The platform supports planning and documentation. Execution happens outside the platform.

### Does the platform automatically evaluate business ideas?

No. The platform supports validation workflows but does not make business decisions.

### Can one idea have multiple versions?

Yes. The platform is built to keep learning and evolution traceable over time.

### When should I use an iteration?

When the overall idea remains promising and you want to build on existing learnings.

### When should I use a pivot?

When key assumptions have been disproven and a different direction should be explored.

## Final Note

The platform does not replace customer feedback or entrepreneurial judgment.

It provides a structured framework to make assumptions explicit, document evidence, and make decisions based on learning rather than guesswork.
